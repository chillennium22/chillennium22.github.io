Chillennium 2017 kept the growing trend, with 310 students participating from 21 schools, including 2 teams from Sheridan College in Canada. This not only made Chillennium 2017 the first international Chillennium, but it also broke the record for the largest student game jam in the world!

2016

Chillennium 2016 saw a huge increase in popularity, with over 250 registered participants. With the games becoming more complicated, including games made for VR, Chillennium 2016 proved the quality of student-made games and secured Chillennium as the largest student game jam in the US.

2015

Chillennium 2015 marked the start of the annual jam, which itself grew out of a game jam hosted by the Texas A&M LIVE Lab in collaboration with the A&M Department of Visualization. With 35 participants in 12 teams, Chillennium 2015 provided a taste of what was to come.
